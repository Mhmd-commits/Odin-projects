berry compote

description

a dessert originating from medieval Europe, made of whole or pieces of fruit in sugar syrup">

Ingredients

  • 2 cups mixed berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, blackberries)
  • 1/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemon zest (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon water (optional, if needed)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Berries

  1. If using fresh berries, wash and hull them as needed. If using frozen berries, you can use them directly without thawing.

Step 2: Cook the Compote

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the berries, sugar, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the berries release their juices and the mixture starts to thicken, about 10-15 minutes.
  3. If the compote is too thick, you can add a tablespoon of water to thin it out. If it's too thin, continue cooking to reduce it further.
  4. For extra flavor, stir in the lemon zest towards the end of cooking.

Step 3: Cool and Serve

  1. Remove the compote from the heat and let it cool slightly.
  2. Serve warm or chilled over pancakes, waffles, yogurt, ice cream, or desserts. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week.
